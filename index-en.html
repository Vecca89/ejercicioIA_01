Salamanca

World Heritage and City of Knowledge

Where is it located?

Salamanca is located in western Spain, in the autonomous community of Castilla y León. It is the capital of the province of the same name and sits on the banks of the Tormes River, in a plateau environment that gives it its characteristic climate and light.

Essential Monuments

Known as the "Golden City" for the tone of the Villamayor stone, Salamanca is home to some of the most beautiful monuments in Spain.

Plaza Mayor: The heart of the city.

The Cathedral: A unique architectural ensemble.

Alma Mater: University City

The University of Salamanca is one of the oldest in the world. Founded in 1218 by Alfonso IX of León, it has been a lighthouse of knowledge for centuries, attracting students from all corners of the globe, giving the city a vibrant and youthful atmosphere.

Flavors of Salamanca

Salamanca's gastronomy is rich, hearty, and full of tradition. Highlights include products derived from the Iberian pig and dishes that comfort the soul.

  • Hornazo: Meat pie filled with loin, ham, and chorizo.
  • Chanfaina: Rice with blood, lamb pieces, and spices.
  • Farinato: Typical sausage from Ciudad Rodrigo, made with bread and fat.
